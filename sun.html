SUN

A massive, hot ball of plasma!

  • The Sun is approximately 109 times the diameter
    of Earth and contains 99.86% of the total mass of our solar system
  • The Sun is about 4.6 billion years old and is middle-aged
    in terms of its life cycle. It's expected to shine for another 5 billion
    years before evolving into a red giant
  • The Sun's immense energy comes from nuclear fusion in its core,
    where hydrogen atoms combine to form helium, releasing an
    enormous amount of energy in the process
  • It takes about 8 minutes and 20 seconds for sunlight to reach
    Earth, meaning that when we look at the Sun, we're seeing
    it as it was over 8 minutes ago
  • The Sun is primarily composed of hydrogen (about 74%) and
    helium (about 24%), with heavier elements making up only a small
    fraction of its composition
